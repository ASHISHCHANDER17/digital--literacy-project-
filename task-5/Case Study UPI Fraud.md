### **Case Study: UPI Fraud**



UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and first-time digital users. It typically occurs when fraudsters trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent transactions.



A common scenario involves a scammer posing as a buyer or bank representative. For example, a student selling a product online may receive a call from a “buyer” who claims to send money via UPI. Instead of sending money, the fraudster sends a “collect request” and asks the student to enter their UPI PIN to receive payment. Confused users often enter their PIN, unknowingly authorizing a debit transaction.



In other cases, victims receive fake links or messages claiming rewards, cashback, or urgent KYC updates. Clicking these links may lead to phishing websites or malicious apps that steal banking details.



Young users are frequently targeted due to lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and emotional stress.



This case highlights the importance of understanding how UPI works and being cautious while handling unknown requests. Users must remember that entering a UPI PIN always means money is being sent, not received.
